About Hamburg

Hamburg is Germany's second-largest city and one of Europe's most important ports. Located in the north of the country, it sits on the River Elbe and is known for its maritime history, modern architecture, and vibrant cultural scene. The city is famous for the Elbphilharmonie, a striking concert hall, and the historic Speicherstadt, the largest warehouse district in the world. Hamburg also has a lively harbor, called the "Gateway to the World," which has been central to its economy for centuries. Hamburg is also known for its cultural life. The Reeperbahn in St. Pauli is famous for nightlife and music, while theaters, museums, and galleries offer something for everyone. Food lovers can enjoy local specialties like Franzbrötchen, Labskaus, and fresh fish sandwiches from the harbor. With its mix of history, culture, and modern living, Hamburg is a city that offers something unique for tourists and locals alike.


Elbphilharmonie

The Elbphilharmonie is Hamburg's modern landmark, opened in January 2017. The 110-meter high concert hall was designed by Swiss architects Herzog & de Meuron. It combines a historic brick warehouse (Kaispeicher A from 1963) with a stunning glass structure that looks like a wave or crystal. The Grand Hall has 2,100 seats arranged around the stage, so no one sits further than 30 meters from the conductor. Visitors can visit the Plaza at 37 meters height for free – offering a 360° panoramic view of the harbor and city.

Finkenwerder Scholle

Finkenwerder Scholle is a classic fish specialty from the Hamburg district of Hamburg-Finkenwerder. This traditional recipe features a tender, pan-fried whole plaice served with a hearty topping of crispy bread and sautéed onions. The combination of mild, delicate fish and rich, savory flavors makes it one of North Germany's most beloved regional dishes. It is typically accompanied by boiled or fried potatoes and a staple of coastal cuisine.

Cultural Heritage

The Speicherstadt is Hamburg's historic warehouse district and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built between 1883 and 1927, these red-brick Gothic buildings on canals once stored coffee, tea, and spices from around the world. Today, it's a unique mix of history, museums, and modern attractions – the heart of Hamburg's maritime heritage.
